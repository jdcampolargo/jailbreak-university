# Philosophy

Jailbreak University is not a productivity brand.
It is a way of seeing college.

## The worldview

- The university is a system, not a god.
- Projects are the real classroom.
- Interesting people matter more than prestige scripts.
- Curiosity should lead action, not just private feelings.
- Friendship compounds through repeated contact.
- Bureaucracy is human and therefore gameable.
- Students have more agency than they think.
- College should not collapse into grades, internships, and credential theater.
- The point is to become more alive, not more compliant.

## What this stack tries to do

It should help a student:

- get unstuck
- make a real move
- build something with other people
- learn the rules well enough to bend them when appropriate
- find food, money, friends, leverage, and truth
- use campus as a place to create, not just consume

## What it rejects

- passive advice
- performative hustle
- campus wellness fog
- career theater
- fake rebellion
- treating every problem like a personal failure

## Manuscript energy to preserve

The manuscript keeps returning to a few moves.

- Look at the institution and ask what it is really doing.
- Build the thing instead of waiting for permission.
- Treat weird campus opportunities as openings, not distractions.
- Use projects to find friends, leverage, and direction.
- Stop acting like college is supposed to hand you a life.

## If the stack works

A student should leave with one of these after using it:

- a concrete plan
- a draft they can send
- a project wedge
- a relationship move
- a records request
- a better class decision
- a cleaner sense of what to do this week

If it only produces reflection, it failed.
