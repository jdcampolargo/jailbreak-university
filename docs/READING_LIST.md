# Reading list

This is not homework. It is a way to get more alive.

Read to change your judgment, not to collect clever opinions.

## How to use this list

- read one essay, one book, and one archive at a time
- reread things that make you feel a little more dangerous to dead systems
- take notes on moves, not summaries
- if a text does not change how you act, it is probably not the right text yet

## Starter stack

### Essays

- `You and Your Research` by Richard Hamming
- `The Tinkerings of Robert Noyce`
- `How to Start a Startup` if you want to understand how builders think
- a few good profiles of people who actually made something

### Books

- `How to Read a Book`
- `The Death and Life of Great American Cities`
- `The Structure of Scientific Revolutions`
- `The Whole Earth Catalog` as a way of thinking, not a museum piece

### Archives

- a local campus catalog
- a campus newspaper archive
- old syllabi from classes you care about
- a local history archive or city newspaper archive
- public records logs and FOIA releases

### Biographies

- people who built weird useful things
- people who fought institutions without becoming fake martyrs
- people who learned by doing, not by announcing themselves

## Reading rituals

- one long read every week
- one short read every day
- one walk after reading so the ideas have somewhere to go
- one note to yourself about what move the text suggests

## What this should do

- widen your taste
- sharpen your judgment
- make interesting people easier to recognize
- give you material for better essays, better projects, and better conversations

## What this should not do

- turn into prestige cosplay
- turn into a list you never touch
- become another way to procrastinate real work
