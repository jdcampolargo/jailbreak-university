# Routing

Route by bottleneck, not by topic label.
The question is not "what area of student life is this?"
The question is "what move gets this student less trapped and more alive fastest?"

## Primary front doors

Use these before anything narrower when the student is still in the fog.

- `/jailbroken`: the student is overcompliant, trapped in default scripts, prestige theater, or obedient drift and needs a sharper life design reset
- `/make-me-alive`: the student is bored, numb, lonely, uninspired, or socially half-dead and needs aliveness through projects, people, rituals, or environments
- `/office-hours`: the student has multiple problems at once, cannot name the real bottleneck, is emotionally flooded, or does not know where to start
- `/opportunity-audit`: the student has a shiny option in front of them and needs to know whether it is actually good, just prestigious, or actively deadening
- `/status-detox`: the student cannot tell what they want because comparison, prestige hunger, or inner-ring behavior is contaminating the signal

## Pattern routing

When the user says:

- "I am doing the right things and still feel dead" -> `/jailbroken`
- "I am functional but numb, lonely, or bored" -> `/make-me-alive`
- "Everything is tangled and I do not know where to start" -> `/office-hours`
- "This looks good on paper, but I do not know if it is actually good" -> `/opportunity-audit`
- "I cannot tell what I want versus what would impress people" -> `/status-detox`
- "I hate my major but do not know what replaces it" -> `/what-to-study` or `/design-your-own-major`
- "I want something to obsess over" -> `/follow-curiosity`
- "I want to get smarter for real" -> `/reading-list`
- "I want to make something useful" -> `/build-project`
- "I want a campus publication, account, or media project" -> `/campus-newspaper` or `/publish-something-real`
- "I want to understand how this institution actually works" -> `/foia-campus`
- "I need the exact rule, date, or office" -> `/bureaucracy-game`
- "I need relief this week" -> choose the skill that ends in a real move this week, not the most prestigious one

## Routing bias

When in doubt:

- start with the skill that names the real trap, not the prettiest category
- choose the skill that ends in a real move this week
- once the project type is obvious, move to the narrower execution skill
- if the user is hiding in vague language, interrupt it
- do not keep the user in diagnosis longer than needed
- do not assume one campus, one bureaucracy, or one culture; write for portable patterns
