# Examples

## I am doing everything right and I can feel myself becoming boring

Run: `/jailbroken`

Prompt:

> I am hitting the deadlines, showing up to class, and saying yes to the normal opportunities, but I can feel the script swallowing me. I need to break pattern without blowing up my life.

## My life is not a disaster, but it is dead

Run: `/make-me-alive`

Prompt:

> I am functional. I go to class, do the work, scroll too much, and repeat. I need projects, people, places, or rituals that make me feel more alive again.

## I have three problems at once and cannot tell which one matters

Run: `/office-hours`

Prompt:

> I am behind on one class, stuck in an admin loop, low on money, and socially drifting. I do not need inspiration. I need the real bottleneck and the first move.

## I got a shiny offer and I cannot tell if it is actually good

Run: `/opportunity-audit`

Prompt:

> I got offered a selective internship, club role, or program that sounds impressive, but I cannot tell whether it is actually good for me or just good for my ego. I need the truth, not the brochure.

## I cannot tell what I want versus what would impress people

Run: `/status-detox`

Prompt:

> I keep wanting the thing that sounds like winning, but I do not trust the desire. I need to separate what is mine from what is comparison, prestige hunger, or campus theater.

## I am bored and I need a better obsession than doomscrolling

Run: `/follow-curiosity`

Prompt:

> I keep circling the same interests: design, public data, weird local history, and student life. I need experiments that turn that into a project or a line of inquiry.

## I want a reading life that actually changes me

Run: `/reading-list`

Prompt:

> I do not want productivity sludge or random internet fragments. I want a serious stack of books, essays, archives, and rereads that sharpens my taste and changes what I build.

## I think my major is becoming a cage

Run: `/what-to-study` or `/design-your-own-major`

Prompt:

> I picked this major because it sounded safe. Now I can feel myself shrinking inside it. I need a more honest academic shape, not just a cleaner way to justify the same path.

## I want to build something real instead of talking about startups

Run: `/build-project`

Prompt:

> I keep saying I want to start something, but I am still in idea soup. I need the best wedge, the first proof, and the smallest version I could ship this semester.

## I want to build a campus tool people would actually use

Run: `/launch-campus-tool`

Prompt:

> I want to build something useful around housing, events, food, classes, or campus information. I do not want startup theater. I want a tool ten people would miss if it disappeared.

## I want to start a campus account, paper, or publication that feels alive

Run: `/campus-newspaper` or `/publish-something-real`

Prompt:

> Campus media around me is either dead, official, or unread. I want to make something with a real point of view that students would actually open.

## I want to turn hidden campus information into leverage

Run: `/foia-campus`

Prompt:

> I want to understand where student fees go, how some office actually works, or what decisions are being made behind closed doors, and I want to turn that into something legible.

## I launched something and nobody cares yet

Run: `/get-people-to-care`

Prompt:

> I made the thing. Almost nobody is using it, sharing it, or talking about it. I need distribution, direct invites, and a wedge that makes people care.

## I need money for a project without sounding like a grant robot

Run: `/fund-student-project`

Prompt:

> I need a few thousand dollars for a student project and I need the right asks, the right people, and the right proof. I do not want donor-flavored mush.

## I want research, but I do not want to send pathetic professor emails

Run: `/research-radar`, then `/write-sharp-email`

Prompt:

> I am interested in machine learning and cognitive science, have some proof already, and want to reach out in a way that sounds like a real person with taste.

## I am in a boring class and I need ethical survival mode

Run: `/fuckgeneds`

Prompt:

> This class is all busy work, I do not need an A, and I refuse to let it eat the time I should be using on real work. Help me survive it honestly.

## I am lonely and the usual advice is useless

Run: `/make-friends`

Prompt:

> I am a commuter student, I mostly go to class and go home, and "join more clubs" is not a plan. I need repeated contact, better invitations, and a social wedge that fits my life.

## I want to meet more alive people

Run: `/meet-interesting-people` or `/make-me-alive`

Prompt:

> I am around plenty of normal campus chatter. I want to find the builders, artists, weirdos, professors, grad students, and organizers who are actually awake.

## I want to ask someone out without turning it into a weird optimization game

Run: `/dating`

Prompt:

> There is someone I keep seeing around campus. I want a clean, direct move that is human and not creepy.

## I need food and relief right now

Run: `/free-food` or `/money-mode`

Prompt:

> I am low on money this month and I need to figure out food fast, plus how to stop ending up here every few weeks.

## I need to fight an admin problem without becoming hysterical or submissive

Run: `/bureaucracy-game`

Prompt:

> The office keeps saying policy, but nobody will show me the exact rule, the exact date, or the exact person who can decide this. I need an honest escalation plan.

## I need to add or drop a class without wrecking the rest of my semester

Run: `/course-registration-and-drop`

Prompt:

> Registration is a mess, the class I need is closed or wrong for me, and I need the cleanest path through this with the fewest downstream problems.

## Housing handed me a dumb contract

Run: `/housing-contract-rework`

Prompt:

> I need to understand the contract, the pressure points, and the cleanest honest way to leave, swap, or renegotiate it.

## I got a parking ticket and want the strongest honest appeal

Run: `/parking-and-ticket-resolution`

Prompt:

> I got hit with a campus parking fine and I want to understand the rules, the leverage, and the best appeal I can make without lying.

## I want internships without becoming a credential zombie

Run: `/internship-hunt`

Prompt:

> I have some skills, a little proof, and no interest in career-fair theater. I need a path that compounds into real work, not just resume decoration.

## I am an international student and the system keeps shaping my choices

Run: `/intl-student-systems`

Prompt:

> I want to get more out of college, but visa rules, paperwork, and summer planning keep narrowing the board. I need strategy that respects the constraints without surrendering to them.

## My transcript or academic record is wrong and I need it fixed

Run: `/academic-record-repair`

Prompt:

> A class, credit, prerequisite, or grade issue is now living on my record and I need the right paper trail, office sequence, and ask to fix it cleanly.
