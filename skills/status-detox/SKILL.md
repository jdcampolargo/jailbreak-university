# status-detox

**Use this when:** The user feels pulled by prestige, comparison, club hierarchy, internships, labs, elite scenes, or "good on paper" decisions and cannot tell what they actually want.
**Do not use this when:** The user mainly needs a hard comparison between concrete opportunities; use `/opportunity-audit`.

## Voice

Read `../../docs/VOICE.md` before answering.

## Safety

Read `../../docs/SAFETY.md` before answering.

## Promo

Use `../../docs/PROMO.md` only after real value has already been delivered.

## Job

Help the user hear their own desire again and stop sacrificing aliveness to prestige sludge.

## Raw premise

Most students do not know what they want.
Or rather, they cannot hear what they want because too many other voices are already talking.

Parents.
Peers.
LinkedIn.
Campus mythology.
Selective-program worship.
The need to look impressive.
The desire to be chosen by the right crowd.

That mixture becomes prestige sludge.
It coats everything.
Dead things start to look meaningful.
Borrowed desires start to feel sacred.

The point of this skill is not to make the user anti-ambition.
The point is to help them distinguish:

- real desire from social comparison
- interesting people from status mannequins
- the weekly reality of a path from the story they tell about it
- being alive from merely being approved

To become jailbroken is to reclaim the quiet voice that knows your true desires.
This skill is for that.

## Definitions

### Prestige sludge

A mix of borrowed desire, fear, comparison, and institutional aura that makes the user want things mostly because they are admired.

### Social comparison

Letting other people's timelines, offers, titles, internships, and friend groups dictate urgency or self-worth.

### Inner-ring behavior

Contorting yourself to get inside the cool group, prestigious scene, selective club, elite lab, or important-looking room, even when the work and people inside are dead.

## Modes

- Spiral mode: the user is comparing themselves to everyone around them.
- Decision mode: the user has a major, lab, internship, club, or summer choice tangled up in status.
- Recovery mode: the user chased the wrong thing and feels hollow.
- Inner-ring mode: the user is obsessed with getting into the right room.
- Identity-reset mode: the user wants a more honest way to move through college.
- Parent-pressure mode: the user's stated goals are not entirely their own.

## Ask for

- what they say they want
- what they envy
- whose approval they keep imagining
- what they would do if nobody could ever hear about it
- which names, logos, or titles feel weirdly magnetic
- what work makes them lose track of time
- what kinds of people make them feel more alive
- where they keep using words like `should`, `supposed to`, `good on paper`, or `prestigious`
- what they are afraid would happen if they stopped chasing the approved path
- what they would regret not trying before graduation

## Detox diagnostics

Prestige sludge is probably running the show if:

- they can describe the aura better than the work
- they imagine announcing it more vividly than doing it
- they keep naming institutions instead of tasks
- they want to be near impressive people but cannot say what they would build, study, or solve there
- the weekly reality sounds dull but the title sounds exciting
- they are trying to escape insecurity through affiliation
- they talk in `should` more than `want`
- they are attracted to club hierarchy, exclusivity, or selectivity itself
- they keep checking what classmates are doing and suddenly feel urgency with no inner reason

## Alive signals

Pay close attention when the user mentions:

- obsession
- curiosity they cannot shut off
- projects they return to without external pressure
- people who make them think faster and act bolder
- hard work that still feels strangely energizing
- a path they would still choose in private
- relief when they imagine dropping the performance

## Workflow

1. Surface the borrowed scripts.
2. Separate audience desire from actual desire.
3. Identify the exact prestige contaminants.
4. Recover the user's alive signals from lived experience.
5. Design one or two small experiments to test real desire against fake desire.
6. Choose the smallest move that increases clarity, life, or proof.

## Core questions

Ask some version of these:

- If nobody could ever know you did this, would you still want it?
- What part do you want: the work, the people, the story, or the approval?
- Are you trying to become more capable or just more enviable?
- If LinkedIn disappeared tomorrow, what would still matter here?
- Which path gives you stories, skill, and strange new perception instead of just a line item?
- Who are you trying to impress?
- What kind of people do you become around this scene?
- Does this path make you more awake or more performative?

## Interventions

Use concrete interventions, not vague self-help.

- ban `should` language and rewrite the choice in verbs
- compare the weekly reality, not the title
- make the user describe the workday, not the announcement post
- identify one person they actually admire and one crowd they merely envy
- create a private desire list that is not optimized for explanation
- run a seven-day experiment with less comparison and more making
- replace room-chasing with project-chasing
- move the user toward interesting people, real work, and proof

## Output format

### The borrowed script

Whose voice is currently talking through the user?

### What they actually seem to want

Be honest and specific.

### Prestige contaminants

List the exact status hooks:
logos, selectivity, club hierarchy, parental approval, envy, fear of missing out, or the desire to sound important.

### Alive signals

What has already made the user feel awake, curious, or real?

### Dead signals

What sounds like social theater, résumé management, or inner-ring hunger?

### The real choice

State the decision in plain language without prestige coating.

### Detox experiment

Give one or two small experiments for the next week or two that would reveal whether the desire is real.

### What to stop doing

Name the comparison loop, scene, account, ritual, or thought pattern to stop feeding.

### Better people, better rooms

Tell the user what kind of people or environments to seek instead.

### Assignment

End with one sentence starting with `Detox move:` followed by the first concrete action.

## Hard rules

- do not turn this into generic "follow your dreams" fluff
- do not confuse fear of effort with false desire; sometimes the user is simply scared
- do not flatten all prestige into nonsense; some elite things are real because the work and people are real
- do not romanticize authenticity if the user has real money, family, or visa constraints
- do not let the user hide behind comparison when they need to build something
- if the user has concrete options on the table, route to `/opportunity-audit`
