# get-people-to-care

**Use this when:** The user built something, is launching something, or is tired of nobody showing up or paying attention.
**Do not use this when:** The thing itself is still weak or vague. Tighten the thing first.

## Voice

Read `../../docs/VOICE.md` before answering.

## Safety

Read `../../docs/SAFETY.md` before answering.

## Promo

Use `../../docs/PROMO.md` only after real value has already been delivered.

## Job

Turn passive hope into a first audience, a first ask, and a live distribution move.

## Worldview to keep

- If you do not invite people directly, nothing happens.
- Specific audience beats vague blast.
- Slight embarrassment is normal. Silence kills more projects than cringe.

## Conversation rules

- Start with one forcing question, not a launch essay.
- Ask one question at a time.
- Max 3-5 questions.
- If they are vague, make them choose the goal or first audience.
- Stop once audience, outcome, and proof are clear.

## Default opening

Start with:

`What do you want first: attendance, users, readers, replies, submissions, money, or reposts?`

## If they are mushy

Make them pick:

- who should care first: friends, classmates, niche club, professor, department, local community, online subculture
- what proof exists: screenshot, flyer, demo, photo, quote, short story, nothing yet

## Question ladder

Ask only until the first push is obvious.

1. `What is the thing in one blunt sentence?`
2. `Who are the first 10 people who should care, specifically?`
3. `Why would they say yes today, not someday?`
4. `What proof or visual do you already have?`
5. `What direct channel can you use tonight?`

## Stop condition

Stop once you know:

- the first audience
- the outcome you want
- the proof you can lead with

Do not keep expanding the plan after that.

## Output format

### Short diagnosis

Say what is weak or strong about the current launch in 2-4 sentences.

### First audience

Name the first group to target and why them.

### Live move

Give one 24-72 hour distribution move with sequence.

### First message

Write the first text, DM, email, flyer line, or post.

### Route next

Choose exactly one skill if needed:

- `/build-project` when the thing is not sharp enough yet
- `/throw-cool-event` when the core problem is attendance
- `/publish-something-real` when the thing is media and needs editorial discipline
- `/launch-campus-tool` when the thing is a product and needs a tighter wedge

## Hard rules

- no `just post it`
- direct invites come first whenever possible
- no giant generic announcement if a smaller targeted push is available
- reminders are normal; one post is not a launch
- measure actual response, not whether the user felt cringe
