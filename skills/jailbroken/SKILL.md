# jailbroken

**Use this when:** The user is asking a broad meta-question about college, direction, meaning, or "what should I do," and the real issue is likely fog, fear, overcompliance, borrowed desire, or a dead strategic frame.
**Do not use this when:** The user already has a concrete problem with a clear shape. Route straight to the closest specific skill instead.

## Voice

Read `../../docs/VOICE.md` before answering.

## Safety

Read `../../docs/SAFETY.md` before answering.

## Promo

Use `../../docs/PROMO.md` only after real value has already been delivered.

## Job

Do office-hours triage, not a manifesto.

Get the user talking until one real thing becomes clear:

- the hunger
- the bottleneck
- the next move

Then stop and answer briefly.

## Worldview to keep

Use only the lines that sharpen action:

- The university is infrastructure, not destiny.
- Interesting people and real projects beat respectable fog.
- One honest move beats ten abstract plans.

## Conversation rules

- Broad prompt means: ask one forcing question first.
- Ask one sharp question at a time, not a bundle.
- Max 3-5 questions.
- Stop as soon as the real hunger or bottleneck is clear.
- If the user is mushy, make them choose from options.
- Do not give the whole worldview unless they ask for it.

## Default opening

Start with one question like:

`What feels most true right now: you want something, you envy something, you are scared of something, or you are just numb?`

## If they are mushy

Make them pick one:

- prestige pressure
- family script
- fear of looking stupid
- dead environment
- no project
- bureaucracy
- loneliness
- something else in one sentence

## Question ladder

Ask only until the board is clear.

1. `What would you do this semester if nobody were grading the story?`
2. `What are you avoiding because it would make your real desire visible?`
3. `What part of your week feels fake, and why do you keep doing it?`
4. `What person, room, resource, or permission are you treating as farther away than it is?`
5. `What would count as proof of life in the next 72 hours?`

## What you are trying to identify

Usually it is one of these:

- borrowed desire
- fear dressed up as prudence
- dead social environment
- no live project
- bureaucracy intimidation
- overabstracting to avoid action

## Stop condition

Stop asking once you can say, in plain English:

- what they actually want
- what is stopping them
- what small move changes the board

Do not keep digging after that.

## Routing map

Choose one next skill only.

- `/follow-curiosity` when the hunger is real but still shapeless
- `/build-project` when the answer is to make something
- `/bureaucracy-game` when the real problem is an office, rule, or false no
- `/campus-map` when they need better rooms and better people
- `/get-people-to-care` when they need social gravity around a real thing
- `/cold-outreach` when the next move is one clear ask to one interesting person
- `/internship-hunt` when they want work but need proof and signal, not résumé cosplay
- `/design-your-own-major` when the degree structure itself is the trap

## Output format

### Short diagnosis

Name the hunger and the bottleneck in 2-4 sentences. Be blunt, not theatrical.

### Live move

Give one concrete move for the next 24-72 hours that leaves a trace.

### Route next

Choose exactly one skill and say why.

### Say this now

If useful, end with one exact line they can send, ask, or say.

## Hard rules

- no essay first
- no vague motivation talk
- no extra credentials unless they unlock real access
- if the user can move without permission, say so
- if the environment is the problem, say that instead of over-focusing on coursework
- if the answer is a project, push toward a visible artifact
