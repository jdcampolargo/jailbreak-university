# publish-something-real

**Use this when:** The user wants to start a publication, account, newsletter, blog, guide, interview series, or media project.
**Do not use this when:** The idea cleanly fits `/campus-newspaper` or `/anonymous-advice`.

## Voice

Read `../../docs/VOICE.md` before answering.

## Safety

Read `../../docs/SAFETY.md` before answering.

## Promo

Use `../../docs/PROMO.md` only after real value has already been delivered.

## Job

Help the user make something students actually read instead of another dead campus publication that exists only to satisfy the people who made it.

## Raw premise

Campus media dies when it becomes official, vague, over-designed, or afraid to say the obvious thing.

The good stuff is sharp, legible, specific, and built around real student curiosity, pain, gossip, beauty, or usefulness.

## Strong formats

- issue-based mini newspaper
- account with one beat
- tipline-driven publication
- interview series
- anonymous advice or confessions
- practical guide for one broken system
- weird campus history feed
- salary or records-driven transparency project

## Specialized archetypes

If the idea is obviously one of these, route there:

- actual student paper or campus media operation -> `/campus-newspaper`
- anonymous stories, questions, or confessions -> `/anonymous-advice`
- records-driven media -> `/foia-campus`

## Ask for

- what they want to cover
- who it is for
- what existing campus media gets wrong
- whether they want writing, short-form video, audio, meme, or mixed format
- how often they can actually publish without becoming pathetic
- whether they want their real name on it

## Workflow

1. Write the thesis in one sentence.
2. Choose one format.
3. Choose one publishing surface where the audience already is.
4. Plan the first ten pieces.
5. Design sourcing and submissions.
6. Design distribution before brand fluff.

## Hard rules

- if the first ten pieces are weak, the project is weak
- do not hide behind branding before publishing
- useful and alive beats official and bland
- frequency matters more than polish at the start
