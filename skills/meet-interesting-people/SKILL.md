# meet-interesting-people

**Use this when:** The user wants better people around them, more alive conversations, collaborators or mentors, or escape from dead social loops.
**Do not use this when:** The real problem is generic loneliness with no specific target; use `/make-friends`.

## Job

Get the user into real proximity with the kinds of people who make them think bigger.

## Raw premise

Interesting people are not mythical.
They cluster around work, obsession, responsibility, and scenes with actual stakes.

The problem is usually one of four things:
the user is vague about who they want,
they keep going to dead habitats,
their questions are flat,
or they never follow up.

## People worth finding

- professors, grad students, and staff who are still alive inside
- students building strange useful things
- artists, writers, filmmakers, musicians
- organizers, archivists, journalists, tinkerers, weird business owners
- people who know everyone and still have taste
- people with a live obsession, not just a status costume

## Ask for

- what kinds of people they want more of
- whether they want peers, mentors, collaborators, or all three
- where they already spend time
- what city, school, or radius they can actually move through
- how much initiative they are willing to take
- what has been failing so far

## Operating procedure

1. Define the person-type in plain language.
2. Map the habitats where those people naturally cluster.
3. Pick one room, one person, and one message for the next 72 hours.
4. Use questions that invite aliveness instead of resume recital.
5. Follow up with a reason to reconnect.
6. If the environment is thin, build the room yourself.

## Strong habitats

- office hours, labs, studios, workshops
- lectures, readings, screenings, and thesis shows
- maker spaces, newspapers, archives, libraries, and community radio
- hack nights, mutual-aid groups, volunteer ops, neighborhood meetings
- niche classes, rehearsals, editing rooms, build rooms
- one cafe, bar, courtyard, or corner where the same alive people keep resurfacing
- online groups with a real local spillover

## Better questions

Ask questions that reveal obsession, judgment, and lived process.

- `What are you working on that actually has your attention right now?`
- `What is the most underrated thing in this department or town?`
- `What rabbit hole have you fallen into recently?`
- `What do people here pretend to care about?`
- `If you had one free month, what would you build or investigate?`
- `Who around here is doing something genuinely interesting?`

## Output format

### Who you are actually trying to find

### Where they probably are

Rooms, roles, events, corners, scenes.

### Next 72 hours

Concrete moves only.

### Conversation opens

Give 5 to 10.

### Follow-up lines

Text or email versions.

### How to turn one encounter into real proximity

Show the sequence: good question, small follow-up, second encounter, lightweight invite.

### If the scene is too thin

How to widen the radius, go sideways into nearby scenes, or host a room yourself.

## Trap warnings

- do not wait for interesting people to adopt you
- do not confuse status with aliveness
- do not ask dead questions if you want live answers
- one good conversation is not a relationship; follow up
