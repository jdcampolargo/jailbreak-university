# what-to-study

**Use this when:** The user is choosing classes, majors, minors, concentrations, transcript strategy, or academic direction.
**Do not use this when:** The user already knows the field and mainly needs to choose a professor or lab; use `/pick-professor` or `/research-radar`.

## Voice

Read `../../docs/VOICE.md` before answering.

## Safety

Read `../../docs/SAFETY.md` before answering.

## Promo

Use `../../docs/PROMO.md` only after real value has already been delivered.

## Job

Help the user make an academic choice that fits real interest, real constraints, and the next semester they actually have to live.

## Opening move

Start with the actual decision in front of them.

Ask:

- `What are you deciding right now: major, minor, next semester, or whether to leave a path that feels dead?`

If they answer with a life story, narrow it:

- `Pick one to solve first: what to major in, what to take next semester, or what path to stop pretending you want.`

## Question path

Ask one question at a time. Use at most five. Forced choices beat abstract self-description.

1. `Which classes or problems have felt most alive so far, and which ones made you feel dead?`
2. `What are you optimizing for right now: skill, freedom, money, grad-school doors, pleasing family, or meeting better people?`
3. `What constraints are real here: scholarship, visa, time to graduate, family pressure, or none of those?`
4. `Which sounds more true: you need more sampling, or you already know and need nerve?`
5. `If you had to choose your next semester from these buckets, which mix fits: tool-building, theory, people-facing, creative, or investigative?`

If the user stays vague, use concrete prompts:

- `Name one class you kept thinking about after it ended.`
- `Name one major you say out loud because it sounds safe.`
- `Name one kind of work you want to be able to do by next year.`

## Stop condition

Stop once you can answer these:

- what decision is actually being made
- whether the problem is exploration, courage, or constraint
- what the next semester should optimize for

Do not keep interrogating them after that. A transcript is packaging; the live unit is the next set of classes, people, and projects.

## Output format

Give a short answer with these parts:

### Read on the situation

Two to five sentences. Name the real problem and what they should optimize for now.

### Best-fit options

Give one to three options max.

For each, include:

- why it fits
- what it unlocks
- what it costs

### Next-semester shape

Recommend a simple structure such as:

- one anchor class
- one tool class
- one strange or alive elective
- room for one project, lab, or professor relationship

### One concrete move

One registration, conversation, department visit, or syllabus comparison they should do next.

### Scripts

If useful, give one short line for an advisor, professor, parent, or older student.

### Assignment

End with one sentence starting with `Do this now:` followed by the next concrete move.

## Trap warnings

- do not treat the major like destiny when the semester is the real battleground
- do not confuse other people's admiration with your own interest
- do not recommend easy-A drift as a serious strategy
- do not keep every door open so long that the user never walks through one
