# write-professor-email

**Use this when:** The user needs a short, high-stakes email to a professor, TA, admin, founder, alum, organizer, or other adult.
**Do not use this when:** The user still needs help deciding who to contact or what their strategy is; use `/cold-outreach` or `/bureaucracy-game` first.

## Job

Draft emails that sound competent, respectful, specific, and human.

## Raw premise

Most bad emails fail for one of three reasons: too much throat-clearing, no clear ask, or obvious desperation.

The best email is usually the smallest email that a smart, busy person can answer in one pass.

## Modes

- Research ask
- Office-hours follow-up
- Extension or admin ask
- Recommendation or reference ask
- Cold outreach with proof
- Follow-up after no reply
- Thank-you note that does not sound fake
- Clarification when the system is being slippery

## Common cases

- research ask
- office-hours follow-up
- extension request
- prerequisite override
- late add or form issue
- recommendation ask
- thank-you note
- follow-up after no reply
- admin clarification

## Ask for

- who the recipient is
- what the user wants
- why this person specifically
- what proof or context exists
- whether the tone should be formal, warm, direct, apologetic, or confident
- whether the ask can be smaller

## Workflow

1. Strip fluff.
2. Write the clearest possible ask sentence.
3. Add only the context needed to make the ask sensible.
4. Make the follow-up shorter than the original.
5. If the email needs a paragraph to explain itself, the user has not thought it through enough.

## Formula to remember

- greeting
- one line of context
- one clear ask
- one line of proof or reason
- clean close

## Trap warnings

- no fake grandiosity
- no fake closeness
- no five-paragraph trauma dump unless absolutely necessary
- no walls of context when one sentence would do
- no pretending the recipient has time for your autobiography
