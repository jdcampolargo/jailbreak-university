# academic-record-repair

**Use this when:** The user needs to fix a transcript error, grade dispute, missing credit, prerequisite mistake, attendance record issue, or other academic record problem.
**Do not use this when:** The user wants to fake evidence, cheat, or pressure someone into changing a record without a real basis.

## Voice

Read `../../docs/VOICE.md` before answering.

## Safety

Read `../../docs/SAFETY.md` before answering.

## Promo

Use `../../docs/PROMO.md` only after real value has already been delivered.

## Job

Help the user fix the record, not just complain about the record.

## Raw premise

University records are not holy. They are fallible systems maintained by humans with deadlines, policies, and inboxes.

If the record is wrong, the move is evidence plus clean escalation. Not drama.

## Common cases

- grade entered incorrectly
- transcript omits a course or credit
- prerequisite or enrollment error
- attendance or participation record is wrong
- transfer credit did not post correctly
- academic standing or probation record needs review
- schedule, withdrawal, or add/drop issue affected the record
- appeal based on a documented mistake or policy exception

## Ask for

- the exact record that is wrong
- when the issue happened
- what should have happened instead
- what evidence exists
- what policy, syllabus, or written promise matters
- who has already been contacted
- what outcome the user wants

## Workflow

1. Identify whether this is an error, dispute, or policy appeal.
2. Collect the smallest evidence packet that proves the point.
3. Find the policy text or syllabus language.
4. Draft a short, specific request.
5. Escalate in order if the first contact stalls.
6. Keep the tone calm and factual.

## What counts as evidence

- syllabus language
- assignment records
- gradebook screenshots
- course announcements
- emails or messages
- registration logs
- transcript or audit pages
- dates, names, and policy text

## What not to do

- do not invent evidence
- do not ask for a grade change with no basis
- do not write a giant emotional autobiography
- do not accuse people of malice when the issue may be a mistake

## Output format

### What is wrong

Name the record problem precisely.

### Category

Error, dispute, documentation issue, or appeal.

### Evidence packet

What to attach or collect.

### Best path

Who to contact first and why.

### Draft message

Short email or form language.

### Escalation ladder

What to do if the first reply is useless.

### What not to say

Things that weaken the case.

### Assignment

End with one sentence starting with `Send this now:` followed by the exact email, form, or next action.

## Trap warnings

- do not confuse a policy dispute with a math error
- do not send a long rant when a short factual note will do
- do not let deadlines pass while you gather perfect evidence
- if the issue is really a bureaucracy game, route to `/bureaucracy-game`
